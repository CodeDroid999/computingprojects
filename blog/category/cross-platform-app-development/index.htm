What is NFC Payment App, How it Works and What are its Benefits?

NFC Payment Apps or Near Field Communication Apps have become very popular now a days and are widely used today in various industries like retail, banking etc. NFC is a simple method of contactless communication of data between two devices which are placed closely i.e within a distance of less than 20 cm. This technology has already been adopted in so many countries across Asia, Europe and North America. NFC mobile payments are used in UK since 2011 hence even if you have never owned an NFC supported smartphone, still you may have used it. NFC technology provides a secure architecture and compatible framework for application provider companies to use the technology for mobile payment capabilities i.e peer to peer money exchange. As NFC apps are secure hence they provide unique opportunity for the Mobile  App Creator or app developer to integrate NFC app payment into their mobile apps as it is highly needed so many times. How Does NFC Payment App Works? NFC has been build upon RFID systems and allows two way communication between endpoints while the earlier systems like contactless smart cards were only one way. NFC has been used in Android devices since Android 4.0 Ice Cream Sandwich version with a name Android Beam which was first launched in Google Nexus smartphones. While processing NFC mobile payments, Android Beam uses NFC to enable Bluetooth on both devices, pairing them instantly and then disabling Bluetooth automatically on both the devices once the desired transaction is completed.
